# Keyword Map & Organic Content Strategy

Non-public reference document for `awakenagain.com` (Amber's Alchemy Apothecary).
This file is never indexed — it's editorial guidance for the humans writing copy,
product pages, herb encyclopedia entries, and articles.

> **Rule of the house**: these keywords are editorial targets, not fill. Public
> pages stay readable, elegant, and on-brand. Never paste keyword lists into
> visible content. Never stuff meta tags. Never create doorway pages.

## 3. Content expansion backlog (only build if ROI holds)

Prioritized list of net-new content. Each entry anchors a single intent and
links back into existing product / encyclopedia pages.

1. **"How to choose your first herbal remedy"** — beginner hub that funnels to
   the Find My Remedy quiz. Answers the "I'm curious but overwhelmed" visitor.
2. **Seasonal landing pages** — one per season (Spring Cleanse, Summer Cooling,
   Autumn Immunity, Winter Rest). Publish 4–6 weeks ahead of each season.
3. **Individual herb entity pages** for the top 30 herbs above. Each page:
   - What it is (botanical + common name)
   - Traditional uses
   - How to take (tea / capsule / tincture)
   - Who should avoid it
   - Products at Amber's that use it
   - 3 related herbs
4. **Concern → formula decision guides** ("Best herbs for sleep if you also
   have anxiety", "Best herbs for PMS if you're caffeine sensitive", etc.)
5. **Gift guides** — holiday, Mother's Day, self-care, wellness curious friend.
6. **"Meet the founder" article** on the About page — authentic, long-form.
   Supports E-E-A-T for every product page.
7. **Interview series** with the people Amber sources from — supports trust and
   earns natural inbound links from supplier sites.
8. **FAQ expansion** — move from 5 FAQs to 20+, broken into "About the
   products", "About the ingredients", "About ordering", "About herbalism".

## 4. Internal linking rules

- Every product page should link to the 2–3 herbs in its top ingredients, and
  to 1 article that explains the concern it treats.
- Every herb entry should link to 1–2 products that use it and 1 related guide.
- Every guide should link to at least 2 products and 2 herb entries.
- Every FAQ answer that mentions a product should deep-link to it.
- Footer includes links to the encyclopedia, ingredient library, articles,
  custom formula, and soaps (already in place).

## 5. What we do NOT do

- No scraped content
- No auto-generated herb pages from lists
- No keyword stuffing in alt text, hidden divs, or tag clouds
- No "hidden for SEO" content
- No fake reviews / testimonials / ratings
- No cloaking
- No fake "as seen in" logos or press mentions
- No unsubstantiated medical claims (always use "traditionally used to support",
  "may help", etc., and defer to the practitioner for diagnosis)

## 6. Digital PR / authority building — ethical outreach plan

- **Pitch founder story** to Willow & Sage, Herbal Academy blog, Mountain Rose
  Herbs blog, HerbMentor, and regional wellness publications.
- **Offer free expert contributions** to lifestyle sites (Well+Good, mindbodygreen,
  Byrdie) on topics Amber is genuinely expert in — ask for a contributor byline
  with a link back.
- **Build shareable resources**: "Herbalist's guide to [season] wellness",
  "Every herb in your grocery store's tea aisle, explained". Promote to Pinterest
  and private wellness Facebook groups.
- **Podcast guest outreach**: women's wellness, herbal, and self-care podcasts.
- **Partner with complementary small businesses** (yoga studios, therapists,
  midwives) on co-branded gift guides.
- **Source reviews ethically** from real customers; never purchase or fabricate.
